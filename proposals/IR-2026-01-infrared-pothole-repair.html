Scope of Work

  • Method — infrared / thermal repair: The failed area is heated in place with an infrared heater until the existing asphalt is workable to full depth, then raked/scarified, rejuvenated, brought up with fresh hot mix, re-leveled and plate-compacted. The result is a seamless, thermally bonded patch with no saw-cut edges and no cold joint to ravel or let water in.
  • Pricing basis: Flat repair price for the ~1' × 12' (~12 sq ft) failed area as described. Any additional or larger failed areas found on site are quoted for your approval before any added work.
  • Included — no separate charges: crew labor (~2–3 hrs on site), fresh hot-mix add asphalt (~25 lb total), and maltene-based asphalt rejuvenator worked into the reheated asphalt to restore binder and lock out water.
  • Schedule / hours: Single service call, approx. 2–3 hours on site for a repair this size; scheduled at a mutually agreed time. Repair area must be clear of vehicles at arrival.
  • Weather / surface: Pavement must be free of standing water, snow and ice. Infrared works in cooler temperatures than conventional hot-patching, but application is not performed during active precipitation.
  • Warranty: One (1) year against defects in workmanship and materials (see Terms, Article 10).
  • Excludes: Full-depth base or sub-base failure, drainage correction, crack sealing, seal coat and striping. If the base beneath the pothole has failed (pumping, movement, or hole reopening under load), infrared reheat will not hold and a saw-cut full-depth patch will be quoted separately.

Photos

Base failure vs. surface pothole: Infrared is the right, longest-lasting fix when the asphalt itself has failed at the surface. If we open the area and find the underlying base has failed, we'll stop, document it, and quote a full-depth patch as a separate change order rather than heat over a problem that will return.
