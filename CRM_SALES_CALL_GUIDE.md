# RemarkaPave Sales Call Guide
## NEPQ Framework: Need → Estimate → Price → Qualification

---

## **PRE-CALL: Email Review** (2 min)
Before you dial, scan the form submission for:
- **Lot size**: Stalls / sq ft? → Scope estimate
- **Services checked**: Striping only? Sealcoat? Assessment? → Revenue tier
- **Quote preference**: Call me vs. Email me → Urgency signal
- **Town**: Service area match? Remote growth region? → Priority
- **Message**: "ASAP" / "Spring project" / "Annual" → Timeline

**Scoring Quick Reference:**
| Lot Size | Base Revenue | Priority |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| <5,000 sq ft (10-20 stalls) | $300–700 | Standard |
| 5,000–20,000 sq ft | $700–2,500 | **PRIORITY** |
| 20,000+ sq ft | $2,500+ | **URGENT** |

Care plan inquiry (any size) = **HIGHEST PRIORITY** → MRR engine

## **CALL SCRIPT: NEPQ Framework**

### **Opening (30 sec)**
*"Hi [Name], this is Todd from RemarkaPave. I got your quote request for [service]. I'm gonna take 3 minutes to walk through your lot situation and get you a real number. Sound good?"*

---

### **N = NEED (2 min)**
**Goal:** Confirm what they actually need (not what the form says).

**If they said "Line Striping":**
- "When was it last striped?"
- "Full re-stripe, or just the faded parts?"
- "Any ADA stalls that need repainting?"
- → Clarifies scope. Many people think they need striping when they need sealcoat first.

**If they said "Sealcoating":**
- "How long ago was it sealed?"
- "Are there cracks first, or is it just dull?"
- → If cracks → recommend crack fill ($) before seal ($$$).

**If they said "Crack Filling":**
- "How many cracks — hairlines or wide?"
- "Is it affecting water pooling?"
- → Determines if it's a $200 spot job or a $1,500 full pass.

**If they said "RemarkaReport™":**
- "Great choice. When do you want to walk the property?"
- "Is parking tight, or can we do it off-hours?"

**RemarkaReport Upsell Prompt:**
- "If you haven't seen our assessment, it's free with the quote and usually catches things owners miss."

---

### **E = ESTIMATE (2 min)**
**Goal:** Get the info needed to price it.

**Must-Ask Questions:**
1. **"What's the lot size in square feet, or how many stalls?"**
   - If vague ("big"), push gently: "Is it closer to a small shopping center (15,000 sq ft) or a strip mall (40,000)?"
   - Lot size = 95% of striping price.

2. **"Are there any challenges — tight access, overhead lines, gate codes?"**
   - If yes: "+$200–500 friction cost (setup, equipment positioning)."

3. **"Is this the first time you've called, or have you used pavement contractors before?"**
   - If first time: they might not know what "crack filling" costs ($400–2k range).
   - If repeat: "When was the last service, and who did it?" → Helps you position your quality/pricing.

4. **"When do you want this done?"**
   - "This week" = you can quote same-day.
   - "Spring" or "Next month" = you can plan around schedule.
   - "Don't know" = they're kicking tires. Qualify harder.

---

### **P = PRICE (1 min)**
**Goal:** Give a range, not a number. Set expectation for the written quote.

**Pricing Talking Points:**
- *"Striping runs $X per stall, so a 20-stall lot is around $X–Y depending on ADA complexity. I'll get the exact number after I walk it."*
- *"Sealcoat is around $X per 1,000 sq ft. That 20,000 sq ft lot is usually $X–Y, but I'd want to check the condition first."*
- *"Crack fill is $X per stall if it's light damage, up to $X–Y if we need full coverage. Walking it answers that."*
- *"Full re-stripe with ADA and stencils usually runs $X–Y on a standard lot."*

**Never hard-quote over the phone.** Always say: *"I'll get you a written quote with materials and labor broken out — that usually lands in your email by [tomorrow/end of day]. Any questions on the timeline?"*

**Care Plan Pivot:**
- *"By the way, a lot on a care plan (scheduled maintenance) usually costs 40% less per year than reactive repairs. Interested in talking about that after your first quote?"*

---

### **Q = QUALIFICATION (1 min)**
**Goal:** Determine if they'll actually hire you.

**Qualifying Questions:**
1. **"Are you the one making this decision, or do you need to loop in a property manager?"**
   - If property manager needed: "Perfect. I'll email the quote to you, then you can walk them through it. Should I follow up with both of you?"

2. **"When do you typically make decisions like this?"**
   - "I need to see 3 quotes" → They're price shopping. Use ROI story: sealcoat now = avoid $X repaving cost in 2 years.
   - "I want to start in [month]" → They're serious. Lock the calendar.

3. **"If the quote looks good, what's the next step?"**
   - Should come back to you: "I'll call you, and we'll schedule the work."
   - If vague: "I'll probably get other quotes" → Red flag. Pivot: "That makes sense. The thing about striping is the cheap quote usually means cheaper paint and rushed work. Our quote breaks out exactly what you're paying for. When you compare, look at paint grade and crew experience, not just the line item."

## **CALL ROUTING: Three Scenarios**

### **Scenario 1: Large Lot + Care Plan Interest**
**Example:** 25,000 sq ft property, "Tell me about recurring maintenance," chose "call me in 2 hours"

**Your Move:**
- Lead with: *"You're the kind of property manager we love working with. Let me explain how a care plan saves you stress AND money."*
- Walk through Essential / Protect / Total tiers.
- Get commitment: *"Let me email you a care plan proposal with pricing. When can you talk with [property owner/CFO] about this?"*
- **Flag in CRM:** "CARE PLAN PROSPECT — follow up Friday AM"

**Expected close time:** 3–5 days (needs approval)

---

### **Scenario 2: Medium Lot + Urgent ("Call me ASAP")**
**Example:** 8,000 sq ft, sealcoating, chose "call me," message says "Need this done by next week"

**Your Move:**
- Lead with availability: *"Perfect timing. I can get this scheduled for [day]. Here's what I'll do..."*
- Quote aggressively on the call (within range).
- Get the commitment: *"Let's lock you in for [day]. Can I send you a written quote today so there's no surprises?"*
- **Flag in CRM:** "URGENT — SCHEDULE ASAP"

**Expected close time:** 1–2 days

---

### **Scenario 3: Small Lot + Email Preference**
**Example:** 3,000 sq ft, line striping, chose "email me a PDF quote"

**Your Move:**
- Respect the preference. Keep call brief (2 min max).
- Gather info quickly (size, scope, ADA).
- End with: *"Got it. I'll walk the property and send you a detailed quote by end of day. Any questions, just reply to the email. Sound good?"*
- **Flag in CRM:** "STANDARD QUEUE — email quote, follow up Tue if no reply"

**Expected close time:** 5–7 days (depends on their review speed)

## **Objection Handling**

### **"I'm getting 3 quotes"**
*"That's smart. Here's what to compare: paint grade (we use PROMAR commercial), crew experience, and timeline. Cheap quote usually means cheaper paint and rushed work — you end up restriping in 2 years. Our quote breaks all that out so you can actually compare apples."*

### **"Your price is higher than [competitor]"**
*"Could be. What did they quote you?"* [Listen]
*"Okay. Did they mention [paint grade / crew size / materials warranty]? We run a Titan PowrLiner with commercial paint — that's why our lines last 3+ years. Some crews use a brush. That's the difference."*

### **"Can you do it cheaper?"**
*"Not without cutting corners — and that's not what we do. But I can tell you this: a lot on a care plan actually costs less per year than one-off repairs. Want me to run those numbers?"*

### **"I need to talk to my owner"**
*"Of course. How's best to reach you both? I can send a proposal to both of you, and we can hop on a call together if questions come up."*
